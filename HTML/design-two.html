Prototype Two

Responsive navigation

As previously stated in the design document of prototype one, this website contains a navigation bar on the side of the screen. To keep this consistent, when the website is respondeing to smaller screens, a navigation tab may be opened through use of the burger button on the top left corner. Additionally, it may be closed by a cross button on the top right corner of the navigation panel. The layout of the navigation bar being consistently being on the left hand side, is imperitave in creating a convention within the website. I plan to add a footer at the bottom of the pages, to indicate the end of the page to users, as it may feel like the page has no ending. Furthermore, the cross to close button on the navigation bar and the burger button to open the navigation bar, relies on website conventions.

Font and colours

The font and colour have not changed from prototype one. The font is used primarily for readability and understanding, whilst the colours provide a minimal look which is still appealing and clean. Other colours, which are not black and white, pop out much more in this format, and become much more visually appealing. Additionally, other small changes become more noticable, such as colour changing on hover.

Data visualisation page

In the wireframe for the data visualisation page, there is space for a description of more information about the data graphs. This is important to not only contextualise the information, but also to provide an entry point for users to interact with the page. The colours of the graphs will not be black and white, and will make use of a variety of colours in order to stand out.
